Indoor Positioning System

Leverage real-time indoor location-awareness and positioning to make indoor spaces discoverable

What is indoor mapping?

Indoor positioning systems (IPS) can most easily be explained as GPS for indoor locations. It is a technology that allows users to accurately pinpoint the location of people or assets inside a building using smartphones, mobile devices or other sensors.


How does it work?

Using a variety of internal smartphone sensors, IPS technology detects and tracks data about a device’s location. This location data is ingested by the positioning system generating accurate coordinates which are displayed on an indoor map.

Here are some thing to consider when exploring indoor positioning systems

Superior accuracy

Real-time indoor positioning systems can locate people and objects with an accuracy radius around 2 meters and without latency. So accurate and reliable, organizations can rely on positioning technology to help visually impaired users navigate complex buildings.

Machine learning algorithms

Many positioning systems leverage AI technology to enable and accelerate the positioning setup process. This enables users to quickly create accurate IPS maps eliminating any manual input required to digitally fingerprint an indoor space.

Offline use

Access location based services inside a building regardless of internet connectivity. Maps are downloaded and cached after an initial load for offline use. Positioning calculations are performed on a user’s mobile device through sensor fusion and are accurately provided to the application for use.

Smooth blue dot

Automatic floor transition

Advanced technology automatically detects when a user enters or leaves a building or changes floors, and seamlessly switches over to the relevant map.

Seamless indoor/outdoor transition

By combining outdoor GPS and indoor positioning, you can create full journey experiences from outdoors to inside a building.
